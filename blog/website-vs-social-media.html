Website vs. Social Media: Why Norwegian Businesses Need Both

June 11, 2026 · 5 min read

It's a question we hear often from small business owners: "Do I really need a website? I'm doing fine on Facebook and Instagram."

It's a fair question. Social media is free (or cheap), easy to set up, and millions of Norwegians use it daily. But here's the reality: social media and a website serve completely different purposes, and the most successful businesses use both strategically.

What Social Media Does Well

Social media is excellent for:

  • Building community — engaging with customers, sharing updates, showing personality
  • Driving awareness — reaching new audiences through shares and paid ads
  • Customer interaction — quick questions, comments, and conversations
  • Showcasing daily life — behind-the-scenes content, special offers, events

Think of social media as your storefront window on the high street. It catches people's attention, shows them what's new, and invites them in.

Where Social Media Falls Short

But social media has significant limitations for a business:

  • You don't own your audience. Algorithm changes can reduce your reach overnight. If Facebook decides to show fewer business posts, your followers may never see your content.
  • Limited information. You can't easily share detailed pricing, full product catalogs, or comprehensive service descriptions on social media.
  • Poor SEO. Social media posts don't rank in Google search. When someone searches for your business or services, your posts won't appear.
  • No credibility. A business with only a Facebook page looks less professional than one with a proper website. It suggests the business isn't fully established.
  • Hard to convert. It's difficult to turn a casual social media viewer into a paying customer without a dedicated website to guide them through the process.

What a Website Does That Social Media Can't

A professional website gives you:

  • Full control. You own your site completely. No algorithm can hide you from your customers.
  • Credibility. A well-designed website signals that your business is professional, serious, and trustworthy.
  • 24/7 availability. Your website works around the clock — taking orders, answering questions, and generating leads while you sleep.
  • SEO visibility. A properly optimized website shows up in Google searches, bringing you new customers you never would have reached through social media alone.
  • Rich information. You can showcase your full product range, detailed pricing, customer testimonials, portfolio, and more — all organized and easy to find.
  • Analytics. You can see exactly how visitors find you, what they look at, and where they drop off — data that's invaluable for improving your business.

How They Work Together

The magic happens when you use both together:

  1. Social media drives traffic to your website. Post a teaser on Instagram or Facebook, then link to your site for the full story, product details, or booking form.
  2. Your website converts visitors into customers. A well-designed site makes it easy for interested visitors to take the next step — whether that's making a purchase, booking a consultation, or contacting you.
  3. Both reinforce your brand. Consistent branding across your website and social media builds recognition and trust.

Real-world example: Johnny's Pizza in Fredrikstad uses Facebook for daily updates and Foodora/Wolt for orders — but their website gives customers a complete menu overview, direct ordering info, and a compelling business case for ordering directly instead of through third-party apps. The website builds credibility that a Facebook page alone never could.

The Bottom Line

You don't have to choose between a website and social media — they're not competitors, they're partners. Social media brings people to your virtual door. Your website invites them in and turns them into customers.

In 2026, a business without a website is leaving money on the table. Period.
